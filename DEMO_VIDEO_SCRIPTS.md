# Overlay Demo Video Scripts

## How to use these

These are designed as short, punchy demos you can record quickly for:
- X / Twitter posts
- landing page embeds
- product hunt style clips
- investor demos
- customer outreach follow-ups

Each script includes:
- goal
- target length
- what to show on screen
- voiceover / caption script
- why it works

The strongest pattern for Overlay is:
1. start with a real moment of friction
2. open Overlay
3. show one clean workflow
4. end on saved context or execution

Do not try to show every feature in one video. Overlay gets stronger when each demo tells one clear story.

## Demo 1 — "From thought to action"

Goal: Show Overlay as more than chat: capture, think, and act in one place.

Best for:
- homepage
- top-of-funnel social
- first demo to send investors or prospects

Target length:
- 30 to 45 seconds

What to show:
- start in a normal browser or work context
- open Overlay
- dictate a quick thought with voice
- turn transcript into a note
- ask AI to refine it
- switch to Act mode to do something concrete

Voiceover / captions:

"Most AI tools give you answers. Then you still have to do the work."

"Overlay is different."

"You can speak a thought..."

"turn it into a note..."

"ask AI to refine it..."

"and then switch from Ask to Act to actually move the task forward."

"One workspace to think, remember, create, and act."

Suggested on-screen sequence:
- show messy real-world task: open tabs, notes, messages, etc.
- use voice input: "Draft launch ideas for overlay onboarding"
- save transcript to note
- Ask: "Turn this into a crisp launch outline"
- Act: "Create a follow-up task list for the project"
- show project/note/context saved

Why it works:
- communicates the category fast
- shows the Ask/Act distinction clearly
- ends with persistence, which makes Overlay feel like a workspace not a chatbot

## Demo 2 — "AI that remembers your work"

Goal: Show persistent memory and project context.

Best for:
- website section on memory / knowledge
- outreach to founders, operators, researchers
- retention-oriented messaging

Target length:
- 25 to 40 seconds

What to show:
- open a project in Overlay
- show notes/files/memories already stored
- ask a question that depends on prior context
- get a grounded answer without re-explaining anything

Voiceover / captions:

"The problem with most AI apps is that every session starts from zero."

"Overlay keeps your projects, notes, files, and memories connected."

"So instead of re-explaining your work..."

"you can just ask."

"The workspace already knows the context."

Suggested prompt:
- "What are the top 3 risks in our launch plan based on my notes and project context?"

Suggested ending line:
- "The more you use it, the better it gets."

Why it works:
- memory is hard to explain abstractly but easy to feel in one question
- strong demo for serious users doing recurring work

## Demo 3 — "Ask vs Act"

Goal: Make the product model instantly legible.

Best for:
- product education
- social clips
- onboarding explanation

Target length:
- 20 to 30 seconds

What to show:
- same task in two passes
- first in Ask mode
- then in Act mode

Voiceover / captions:

"In Overlay, there are two ways to work with AI."

"Ask, when you want thinking."

"Act, when you want execution."

"Same workspace. Different intent."

Suggested on-screen flow:
- Ask: "Summarize these customer interview notes into key themes"
- Act: "Turn those themes into a follow-up email draft and save it to the project"

Alternative ending line:
- "Not just answers. Progress."

Why it works:
- simple
- differentiating
- helps viewers understand how Overlay should be used

Goal: Show breadth without becoming chaotic.

Best for:
- 45 to 60 second hero demo
- investor intro clips
- landing page overview section

Target length:
- 45 to 60 seconds

What to show:
- fast cuts across the main surfaces
- chat
- notes
- memories
- knowledge/files
- image/video outputs
- integrations
- browser task or computer

Voiceover / captions:

"Overlay is not another AI chat app."

"It is one connected workspace for real work."

"Chat with top models."

"Write and organize notes."

"Save memory that compounds over time."

"Search your files and knowledge."

"Generate images and video."

"Connect your tools."

"And use AI to browse, execute, and operate."

"One intelligent workspace."

Editing note:
- keep each visual segment under 3 to 4 seconds
- do not narrate every feature in detail
- use this as a category-definition piece, not a deep walkthrough

Why it works:
- useful for people who need the full picture quickly
- best if visually polished and tightly edited

## Demo 5 — "Voice to usable work"

Goal: Show that voice is not a gimmick; it becomes structured output.

Best for:
- social clips
- mobile-adjacent messaging even if recorded on desktop
- users who think out loud

Target length:
- 20 to 35 seconds

What to show:
- record a voice note
- transcribe it
- send it into chat or save it as a note
- turn it into something structured

Voiceover / captions:

"Sometimes the fastest way to work is to talk."

"Overlay turns speech into something usable."

"Record a thought..."

"transcribe it..."

"then turn it into a note, a draft, or the start of a workflow."

Suggested spoken example:
- "Ideas for our next launch: tighter onboarding, referral incentive, founder-led outreach, short demo videos"

Suggested AI follow-up:
- "Turn this into a launch checklist with priorities"

Why it works:
- easy to understand
- feels immediate
- highlights flow and low-friction capture

## Best practices for recording

### 1. Keep each demo about one promise
Bad demo:
- shows 11 features at once

Good demo:
- proves one thing clearly

### 2. Use realistic prompts
Avoid generic prompts like:
- "write a blog post"

Prefer realistic prompts like:
- "Turn these customer calls into launch risks"
- "Draft a founder follow-up based on these notes"
- "Save this voice memo as a note and turn it into a plan"

### 3. Show persistence
Whenever possible, end by showing:
- saved note
- project context
- memory
- output asset
- hosted session

That is what makes Overlay feel durable.

### 4. Keep the UI clean before recording
Before capture:
- close distracting tabs
- preload any needed project context
- make naming consistent
- reduce clutter in the visible workspace

### 5. Record silent first, then add voiceover later
This usually gives a better result than trying to narrate live.
